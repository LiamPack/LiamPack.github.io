The graduate core course in analysis at Cornell. 

Course textbook: [Real Analysis: Measure Theory, Integration and Hilbert Spaces](https://www.amazon.com/Real-Analysis-Integration-Princeton-Lectures-ebook/dp/B007BOK6PW).

Recitation covered applications or extensions of material from class. Some topics:

* Hahn-Banach, Convex Duality ([Brezis; Functional Analysis, Sobolev Spaces and Partial Differential Equations](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-0-387-70914-7) <span>&#167;</span> 1)
* Almost Every Number is Normal via probability ([Kac; Statistical Independence in Probability, Analysis, and Number Theory](https://bookstore.ams.org/car-12/) <span>&#167;</span> 1-2)
* Jordan measure decomposition & Radon-Nikodym
* Some applications of Jensen's inequality
* $(L^p)^* = L^q$, and how a weakly-convergent sequence can fail to converge strongly ([Lieb & Loss; Analysis](https://bookstore.ams.org/view?ProductCode=GSM%2F14.R) <span>&#167;</span> 2)
* Basic $L^2$ theory for $\Delta u = f$ ([Krylov; Lectures on Elliptic and Parabolic Equations in Sobolev Spaces](https://bookstore.ams.org/view?ProductCode=GSM%2F96) <span>&#167;</span> 1)
